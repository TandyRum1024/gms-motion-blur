Introduction

images of my motion blur doing motion blur-y stuff.

Lately, I've been experimenting with visual effects (particularly blurring & procedural animations) a lot.. Combining that with camera's motion informations, I was able to create the camera based motion blurring effect.
Motion blur was one of the many visual effects that inspired me to learn about Shaders, And it's one of my favorite effects because they can enhance the feelings of games by emphasizing its speedy-ness to make the users feel like they're scootin' around the cyberworld at break-neck speed!
So, With the Shaders I made two small projects for (mainly fun and) demonstration purposes.
And in this document (post?), I'll be explaining things about my shader.

The motion blur

image featuring motion vectors being visualized
(left : resulting blurred image, right : sum of motion "offsets" that is used for blurring)

Motion blur is caused by the afterimages from fast movements of object. And for camera based motion blur, We can (cheap and easily) simulate it by applying blurs in various direction according to camera's velocity.
To be more specific, We can apply "linear" blurs for x and y movements of camera, zoom blur for forward/zooming movements of camera and rotational blur for... well, rotating movements of camera.
Normally we can just apply directional blur for x/y velocity then apply zoom & angular blur for zoom and rotational velocity of camera.
But applying blur that way is bit inefficient in my opinion.. What we can do, however is that we can combine the x, y and zoom velocity into one "offset" vector and use that alongside the rotational velocity when we're sampling the screen texture for blur. This way we can calculate the motion blur in only one pass with single blurring procedure!
